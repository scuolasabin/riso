The windows of Zafferano

In 1574, during the construction of Milan's cathedral, the glazier "Valerio di Fiandra" (from the Flandres) and his helpers were in charge of making its windows.
His main helper was "Zafferano" (Saffron), so called because he used the yellow powder to make colours brighter.
For this reason Valerio made fun of him, saying that he would put it anywhere, even into the "risotto".
To get his revenge after years of jokes, at the marriage of his master's daughter, Saffron put the spice into the "risotto".
To tell the truth, he didn't succeed in playing a trick because everybody liked that new "risotto".
This is how "Risotto alla Milanese" was born.
