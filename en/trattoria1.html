Nice to know ...

Every year, rice paddies worldwide use up about 1,350 billion cubic meters of water.
2,300 liters of water are needed to produce one kilo of integral rice.
One grain of rice normally weights 0.019 grams, so one packet of rice (1kg) contains about 52,630 grains.
In several countries, there are a lot of legends about rice: one of these is about the Prophet Mohammed. It tells that a drop of his sweat, falling from the sky, originated a rice plant.
In India the rice is so sacred that before eating it, it is necessary to get purified through ablutions.
Once in India, during funerals, rice was given to crows to protect the souls of the dead.
At the birth of a daughter of a Brahmin, the father asks the other women to leave the room and he puts red rice on his daughter's head to drive away the evil eye.
