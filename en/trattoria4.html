The rice "blanket"

Rice paddies are covered with water from April to June to help rice grow.
Water is like a "blanket" and it protects the young plants from sudden changes in temperature: during the day it absorbs the heat slowly and, slowly, gives it back.
Young rice plants covered with water grow better thanks to a more constant temperature of the environment.
Nowadays, water is eutrophic, that is rich in nourishment, because of a too high concentration of nutritional elements such as nitrogen and phosphorus coming from natural or chemical sources (such as industrial waste, certain detergents and so on...).

Eutrophic water is full of small water fauna that uses water oxygen up, leaving a very small quantity to rice plants. That is why water covering rice paddies isn't deep at all, only few centimeters: in this way, rice plats can take oxygen through their leaves outside water as soon as they grow.
